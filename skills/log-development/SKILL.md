You are helping the user log a professional development activity to track skill building progress.

Development activities include: courses, training, practice, reading, events, mentorship, teaching, or project-based learning.

## Process

1. **Ask what development activity** they completed or are logging:
   - Course or training?
   - Deliberate practice?
   - Book or article?
   - Conference or event?
   - Mentorship session?
   - Teaching others?
   - Project where they built skills?

2. **Gather details:**
   - **What exactly did they do?** [Specific description]
   - **Which skills did this develop?** [Be specific]
   - **How much time?** [Hours invested]
   - **Key learnings?** [What did they take away?]
   - **Where will they apply this?** [Projects, work, situations]
   - **Progress toward goals?** [Does this move any development goals forward?]

3. **Read the development log:**
   - File: `$MYEP_PATH/Craft/development-log.md`

4. **Add the entry** under the appropriate month:

```markdown
### YYYY-MM-DD - [Activity Type]: [Title]

**Skills Developed:** [Specific skills this built]
**Time Investment:** [X hours]
**Format:** [Course/Practice/Reading/Event/Mentorship/Teaching/Project]

**What I did:**
[Brief description of the activity]

**Key Learnings:**
- [Important takeaway 1]
- [Important takeaway 2]
- [Important takeaway 3]

**Application:**
- [How/where they'll apply this]
- [Connection to current projects or work]

**Progress:**
- [Movement toward development goals]
- [Any skill level changes or milestones]

---
```

5. **Update "Last Updated"** date in the header

6. **Suggest connections:**
   - Is this building a skill from their **development-plan.md**?
   - Does this provide evidence for **skills-inventory.md**?
   - Should they update their skill level after this learning?
   - Could this go in **Knowledge Bank** as a resource?

7. **Confirm** the entry has been logged

## Activity Types

Courses, training, practice, reading, events, mentorship, teaching, or project-based learning.

## Guidelines

- **Log immediately** - Don't let them batch, capture right after
- **Be specific** - "Completed module 3 of React course - hooks and state management" not "did some React"
- **Connect to skills** - Always identify which capabilities this built
- **Track time** - Helps understand investment and pace
- **Capture insights** - What's worth remembering?
- **Note application** - Where will they use this?
- **Celebrate progress** - Acknowledge the investment they're making

## Good vs. Vague Entries

**Good:**
"Completed 2 hours of prototyping practice in Figma - focused on micro-interactions and animation timing. Built 5 different button hover states. Key learning: Animation curves dramatically affect perceived quality. Will apply to upcoming dashboard redesign project."

**Too vague:**
"Did some Figma practice. Learned stuff about animations."

**Good:**
"Finished chapters 3-5 of 'Lean Analytics' (3 hours). Key insight: Picking the right metric depends on business stage - we're in product-market fit stage so should focus on engagement over acquisition. Will use framework to audit our current metrics in Q1 project."

**Too vague:**
"Read some of analytics book."

## Encourage Reflection

Beyond just logging, help them reflect:
- What surprised them?
- What challenged their assumptions?
- How does this connect to what they already know?
- What questions does this raise?
- What should they learn next?
